FlowKeep

Privacy Policy

Effective February 12, 2026 · Last updated June 20, 2026 · Developer: Legend Made (com.legend-made.FlowKeep)

Summary: FlowKeep does not sell your personal data, show ads, or run third-party analytics or tracking SDKs, and there are no user accounts to create. Your project data stays on your device and, if you choose, in your own iCloud account. The one exception is the optional Accountability Buddy feature: only if you choose to invite a partner, a small amount of invite data (a random code, the display name you enter, and an anonymous device identifier) is stored on a lightweight backend we operate so the invite can be delivered — see Section 7. If you never use the buddy feature, none of this data is created or transmitted.

1. Information We Collect

We do not collect any personal information. FlowKeep does not ask for your name, email address, phone number, location, or any other personally identifiable information. There is no account creation or login process.

2. Data Stored on Your Device

FlowKeep stores the following data locally on your device to provide app functionality:

DataPurpose
Project titles, descriptions, icons, and colorsDisplaying and organizing your projects
Project notes and linksContext preservation
Touch events (timestamps)Freshness, streaks, and queue priority
User preferencesPersonalizing your experience
Notification settingsDelivering reminders you've opted into
Project templatesBuilt-in and user-created templates
Project dependenciesPrerequisites between projects

All of this data is stored in a local database on your device using Apple's Core Data framework within the app's sandboxed container.

3. iCloud Sync

If you are signed into iCloud, FlowKeep can sync your data across your Apple devices using Apple's CloudKit service. Sync is handled by Apple's infrastructure; data is encrypted in transit and at rest. Your project data goes from your device to your iCloud account only. (The only data FlowKeep ever sends to a server we operate is the optional buddy-invite record described in Section 7.)

See Apple's iCloud Privacy Overview.

4. Notifications

FlowKeep may request permission to send local notifications (freshness reminders, queue alerts). Notifications are optional and no notification data is sent to any external service.

5. Widgets & Siri Shortcuts

Home Screen widgets, Lock Screen widgets, and Siri Shortcuts read project data from a shared on-device App Group container. No data is transmitted externally.

6. Template Sharing

When you share a project template via QR code or link:

  • Template data (title, description, structure) is encoded into the QR code or URL.
  • No data is uploaded to FlowKeep servers (we don't operate any).
  • Shared template links may be hosted on flowkeep.github.io as static GitHub Pages content. These pages do not collect user data or analytics.

7. Accountability Buddy (Optional)

FlowKeep includes an optional Accountability Buddy feature that lets you connect with one partner so they can see your project titles and nudge you when a project goes stale. It is off until you choose to invite someone.

Invite delivery (our backend). When you create an invite, FlowKeep stores a small invite record on a lightweight backend we operate (hosted on Supabase). That record contains: a random invite code; the display name you enter for yourself; an anonymous device identifier (Apple's identifierForVendor) — not your Apple ID, name, email, or phone number; a CloudKit share link; and the invite's status and timestamps. Invite records automatically expire after 14 days. This backend exists solely to deliver invites — it is never used for analytics, advertising, or profiling, and the data is never sold.

The connection and ongoing sync (Apple CloudKit). Once a buddy accepts, the actual sharing — your project titles, freshness status, and nudges — runs through Apple's CloudKit/iCloud, not through our backend.

What stays private. Only project titles and freshness status are visible to a connected buddy. Notes, links, descriptions, time estimates, dependencies, and all other details remain private to you. If you never use the buddy feature, no invite record is created and nothing is transmitted to our backend.

8. AI Features (Disabled)

Optional AI features are currently disabled. No data is sent to Anthropic, OpenAI, Google, or any other AI provider. This policy will be updated before any AI features are enabled.

9. Analytics & Tracking

FlowKeep does not use any third-party analytics or tracking services. No advertising identifiers are accessed. A minimal local event log (last 100 events) is kept on-device for diagnostics only — it is never transmitted.

10. Third-Party SDKs & Services

FlowKeep bundles zero third-party SDKs. No external code from any third party runs inside the app, which is built with native Apple frameworks: SwiftUI, Core Data, CloudKit, WidgetKit, UserNotifications, App Intents, Foundation, and OSLog.

One third-party service is used, and only for the optional buddy-invite feature in Section 7: Supabase, which hosts the backend that stores and delivers invite records. No Supabase code or SDK is embedded in the app — FlowKeep simply makes network requests to that backend when you create or accept a buddy invite. No project content, notes, or links are sent to it. See Supabase's privacy practices.

11. Data Retention & Deletion

Delete the FlowKeep app to remove local data. Manage iCloud storage via iOS Settings if sync was enabled. The only data FlowKeep keeps on a server it operates is the optional buddy-invite record (Section 7), which expires automatically after 14 days. To remove an invite sooner, revoke it in the app or contact us at the address below.

12. Children's Privacy

FlowKeep does not knowingly collect information from children under 13 and is compliant with COPPA.

13. App Store Privacy Label

FlowKeep does not track you across apps or websites and uses no advertising identifiers. If you never use the buddy feature, no data is collected. If you use the optional Accountability Buddy feature, a minimal amount of data is collected solely to deliver invites — an anonymous device identifier, the display name you enter, and the project titles you choose to share — and it is not linked to your identity and not used to track you. Its only purpose is app functionality.

14. Apple Privacy Manifest

FlowKeep includes PrivacyInfo.xcprivacy declaring required API usage (UserDefaults, file timestamps, system boot time, disk space) for app functionality only. NSPrivacyTracking is false.

15. Changes to This Policy

We will update this page and the effective date when practices change, especially before Accountability Buddy or AI features launch.

16. Contact

Questions about this policy or FlowKeep's data practices: info@legend-made.com
